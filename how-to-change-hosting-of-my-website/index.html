How to Change Hosting of My Website

Changing of hosting for website or blog is something which almost every webmasters have to consider Sooner or later in blogging career because of numerous reasons, but many people don’t know the procedure to changing web hosting and just because of it they remain tuned with their old hosting companies even if they not satisfied with their service and offers. So here I’m going to tell you the complete procedure to change hosting company for your website. Here we will discuss how to change your website’s host without affecting SEO rankings of your site. See what you need to do..  

1.

Well it’s obvious if you want to change your hosting then you will need a hosting company that fulfill all your needs & requirements, but finding a perfect hosting provider is not easy. This is because ensuring that the new host has all the necessary features that are required to display your website properly so never take decision in Hurry. Must check how much bandwidth and disk space they offering with a the hosting pack, and check the various services, limitations and the physical characteristics of the server, configuration settings of the server, the time limits, the installed libraries and the version of the server. Choose a reputed company that is known for its quality services. Some best hosting companies are GoDaddy.com, Hostgator, Lunarpages, Network Solutions etc.  

2.

Almost every reputed hosting provider company offers a trial of 1-2 days to ensure that their service is  compatible with your website so test it first, Testing can be done by uploading your database & files on the new host and by checking its functionalities with a temporary URL.   

 3.

Once you final, purchase web hosting package from the company you want. Confirm the purchase and carefully read the emails that you receive from the company. As you can guess, the emails contain sensitive and important information. Taking a printout of it or keeping it in written safe would be a smart thing to do. Once your domain name has been associated with the new web host, you would be able to upload your website on the server.  

4.

Now log in to your domain account and the DNS information in connection with your domain name to the new values. In general, a “Change Name Server” or similar link in your account. Make sure both the primary and secondary name servers. For more information, read how the name servers associated with my domain name.  

5.

If you change the name servers, you would be told that the change will be 24 to 72 hours, so you have to be patient. I noticed that name server changes to take place fairly quickly in just 14-18 hours.  

6.

The information on all domains is stored on some computers around the world. All these machines must be updated with the new information and that is why it take some time go get set, I think you can understand.   Note : The web hosting and the physical location of the server can affect the SEO rankings of the website which means transferring website to other webs hosting is risky and one has to do it with caution.  The above procedure requires that you have basic knowledge of various web technologies and can have serious consequences for your SEO status. It is strongly recommended not to proceed, unless you are tech savvy and you know what you’re doing.  I hope my this post answered all the questions you may have about making the switchover.
