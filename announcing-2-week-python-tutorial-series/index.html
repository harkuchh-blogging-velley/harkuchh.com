Announcing 2 Week Python Tutorial Series

Feb 20th, 2014

Few days back we shared our experience about how we utilize the Python programming language in our work at harkuchh blogging valley. We got very positive interest about it and readers requested tutorials about the Python programming language.

So here I am with the announcement of the python tutorial series on harkcuhh.com.

2 week tutorial series

I have drafted a roadmap for a tutorial of 2 weeks. We’ll publish Python tutorials here and continue for 2 weeks. There will be 14 tutorials, one for each day. You might be thinking if it is possible to master Python in 2 weeks. It is not. Mastering anything takes time. My aim here is to teach you to start thinking in Python. So that you can open any tutorial over there and make sense of it.

I suppose thing which would be rolling in your head would be something like “How much Python will I learn from this tutorial?”. There are not actual metrics to know how much of a language you know, but you will learn enough to continue on your own, or to follow more advanced tutorials that I will do after this one.

This series is meant for absolute beginners, who know little to no programming. We will emphasize more on basics and the thought process one needs to solve problems the Python way.

We will start with a very gentle introduction to the Python programming language, and will raise the bar with every tutorial. We will discuss the general programming problems which are taught in most programming classes, just to showcase how easy it is to do same things with Python. It will enable you to do comparison between other languages and Python if you follow some other course in some other programming language.

My reason for starting this series on harkuchh.com is more than teaching some folks programming. Programming is heavily under-rated in our city Chandigarh. Tech scene in the city is daunting. No enthusiastic community, no user groups, and dominance of PHP. It’s a nightmare. This should change. I want to build a community of programmers. I want to discover the hidden gems in our blogging community. There are many smart people in this community, who I think can make brilliant programmers.

Who’s the targeted audience?

As Krishan has told me, primary readers of harkuchh.com are bloggers, not much acquainted with programming. I want to usher those guys to the world of programming, without being hard on their brains. It will be a fun trip.

How much time you should give to the tutorial?

It will not be a very fast paced or heavily technical tutorial. I am aiming for teaching enough programming that you’ll be able to do some fun things yourself, and will be able to pursue deeper tutorials.

What you will and what you will not learn

You’ll not be able to do magic with just reading these 14 tutorials. At the end of Day 14, you will be able to make sense of any Python tutorial. And depending on how smart you are and with how much dedication you follow this series, you shall be able to automate several of you day-to-day tasks (computer related of course). I won’t make huge promises, but you potential is limited by you only. Work hard, love your work, and conquer the world.

Happy Hacking!
