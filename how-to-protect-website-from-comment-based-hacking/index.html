How to Protect Website From Comment Based Hacking

Hacking, is the most scary word for any blogger or webmaster, we know it takes a lot effort and dedication to make a good blog or website but sometimes people hack them and destroy the all hard work done by a person. Well this is something which not many people are knowing about. Hackers are obviously super genius they are able to effect bigger and biggest websites. Here we are sharing the way from which you can safe your website or blog from Comment based hacking so that not anyone can hack your blog. Read this post carefully.  

What is Comment based Hacking ?

Bloggers are so many but only few knows that,  whatever comment a user make on our website these all gets saved in the database of our website or blog, from where it can be operated via a simple MySql query. So to hack a website hackers and spammers post comments in our website which look completely real, some example of spam messages are :

” Hey nice website I have now bookmarked your website, you really write awesome, I will be waiting for more articles”.

“Wow what a Post, really I can’t believe someone can describe this matter like this, loved it, good work, keep going, now I will check your blog Regularly”.

“You have done a great job, this is one of the best work I’ve seen ever, be regular on updating posts”

After that, Bloggers see these type of comments and and feel happy to approve them. But whenever you approve their comment, they start their operation.  

Issues takes place after that :

You cannot login your admin.

Sitemap disappears.

Internal errors.

Automatic plugins remove.

Posts or categories disappears.

Taking too much time to open post.

How to keep website safe from Comment based Hacking  ?

Here are the few precautions, after following them carefully you need not to worry about at least Hacking by Comments:

comments should be approved, disable the auto approval function from settings.

Install captcha plugin by bestwebsoft.com its name is just “CAPTCHA”.

Never make someone an author, or admin of your website or Blog.

Never approve untrusted users.

Users should be registered to comment, this u can find in general settings.

Don’t approve any comment if it shows  in Spam.
