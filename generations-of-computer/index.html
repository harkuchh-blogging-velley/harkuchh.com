Generations of Computer Detail Information

The development of computers passes through several stages. Each stage is simply known as generations. The following are the generations of Computer :  

1. First Generation Computer (1942-1955)

Wold’s first succesfull Computer was developed by Eckert and J.W. Mauchly. The name of the Computer was ENIAC. Eniac was the beginning of the first generation Computers.These were developed between 1942 to 1955. Vaccum tube swere used in first generation Computers as the electronic Component they were very big in size and their speed was very slow. They were very expensive to operate and in addition to using a great deal of electricity, generated a lot of heat, which was often the cause of malfunctions. the lowest-level programming language understood by computers, to perform operations, and they could only solve one problem at a time. Input was based on punched cards and paper tape, and output was displayed on  printouts.  

2. Second Generation Computers (1956-1964)

These were developed between 1955 to 1964, In second generation Computers transistors were used instead of Vaccum tubes. A transistor is much smaller in size than aVaccum tubeThese computer were faster and smaller. Examples : 1BM 1620, 1BM 1401, 1 BM 7094, UNIVAC 1108.Second-generation computers moved from cryptic binary machine language to symbolic, or assembly, languages, which allowed programmers to specify instructions in words. These were also the first computers that stored their instructions in their memory, which moved from a magnetic drum to magnetic core.  

3. Third Generation Computers (1964-1971)

Third generation Computer were introduced in 1964. In these  IC (Integrated Circuits) were used. The computer had a higher calculating speed, large storage capacity and they were less expensive. They were 10,000 times faster than first generation computers. The famous computer of this generation was 1BM 360 and 1BM 370. Instead of punched cards and printouts, users interacted with third generation computers through keyboards and monitorsand interfaced with an operating system, which allowed the device to run many different applications at one time.

4. Fourth Generation Computers (1971-Present)

These Computers were developed from 1971 onwards. These are present day Computers. In the computer of this generation microprocessor on a single silicon chip were used, as thousands of integrated circuits were built onto a single silicon chip The use of microprocessorrs made them very small, fast and more efficient. After several years In 1981 IBM introduced its first computer for the home user, and in 1984 Apple introduced the Macintosh. Microprocessors also moved out of the realm of desktop computers and into many areas of life as more and more everyday products began to use microprocessors.

5. Fifth Generation Computers ( Present and Beyond )

Fifth generation computers, based on artificial intelligence, are still under development, but there are a number of applications such asspeech recognition, which are used today. The use of parallel processing and superconductors helps artificial intelligence a reality.Quantum computers and molecular and nanotechnology will change the face of computers radically changing in the coming years. The purpose of the fifth generation of computers is the development ofdevices that are responsive to natural language input, and are capable of learning and self-organizations…
